What is the Internet?

Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn recreated the Internet as a result of an experiment called ARPANET, which stood for Advanced Research Projects Agency Network. It was built to be a communications network that could survive a nuclear attack. They build a national packet network and realized it worked and immediately saw how valuable it could to people seeking to share information such as scientists.

Who is in charge of the Internet? The answer to that is everybody and nobody. It is built of a myriad of independently operated networks. There is no central control how packets are routed. Any device can connect to any device, sharing information is the key to global advancement and the Internet is the greatest way to share information.
